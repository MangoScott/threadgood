Our Philosophy

ThreadGrade exists because existing ethical fashion platforms fail the people who need them most: everyday shoppers on real budgets. We don't tell you to stop buying from Target and switch to $150 sustainable tees. We grade the brands you already buy, using publicly verifiable data.

Core Principles

  • Trust-first scoring: If a brand doesn't publish data, they don't get a passing score. Absence of data = cannot verify = low trust.
  • Government data over self-reporting: OSHA violations and CBP forced labor flags carry more weight than a brand's own sustainability report.
  • Budget-inclusive: We rate $8 tees and $150 jackets on the same scale, so you can compare within your budget.
  • No pay-to-play: Brands cannot pay for higher ratings. We are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit.

Three Pillars

Every brand's overall score is a weighted average of three pillars:

Planet 45%
People 40%
Animals 15%

Planet (45%)

Environmental impact across the full product lifecycle. Combines supply chain transparency (WHERE), materials and chemicals (WHAT), and circularity (AFTER) into a single score. This pillar is the average of those three dimension scores.

People (40%)

Labor practices and worker welfare. Directly maps to the WHO dimension. Evaluates living wages, safety records, forced labor risk, freedom of association, and supplier audit transparency.

Animals (15%)

Animal welfare certifications, policies on exotic materials, and environmental protections that benefit wildlife (microplastics mitigation, chemical management).

Four Evaluation Dimensions

Each brand is evaluated on four dimensions which feed into the three pillars above.

WHERE — Supply Chain Transparency

Maximum 100 points. Feeds into Planet pillar.

  • Full factory list published: +20 points
  • Multi-tier supply chain disclosure (3+ tiers): +15 points
  • Open Supply Hub participation: +10 points
  • Sourcing countries disclosed: +5/country, max +20 points
  • Fashion Transparency Index score: scaled to +35 points

WHO — Labor Practices

Maximum 100 points. Feeds into People pillar.

  • Verified living wage commitment: +25 points
  • Supplier code of conduct: +10 points
  • Audit results publicly disclosed: +15 points
  • KnowTheChain benchmark score: scaled to +30 points
  • Clean OSHA safety record: +10 points (violations incur penalties)
  • No CBP forced labor flags: +5 points (flags incur -15 to -30)
  • Freedom of association: +5 points

WHAT — Materials & Chemicals

Maximum 100 points. Feeds into Planet pillar.

  • Sustainable materials percentage: scaled to +25 points
  • High-value certifications (GOTS, OEKO-TEX, Bluesign, GRS): +10 each, max +25
  • Verified certifications from database: scaled bonus up to +25
  • Chemical management policy: +15 points
  • Microplastics mitigation: +10 points
  • Durability commitment: +10 points

AFTER — Circularity & End-of-Life

Maximum 100 points. Feeds into Planet pillar.

  • Take-back/recycling program: +25 points
  • Resale or repair program: +20 points
  • Sustainable packaging: +15 points
  • Circularity goals with timeline: +20 points
  • Design for disassembly: +20 points

Red Flag System

Certain findings trigger red flags that cap scores regardless of other performance. These represent critical ethical violations that no amount of sustainability marketing can offset.

Red Flag Severity Score Impact
CBP forced labor (high risk) Critical People capped at 25, Overall capped at 40
Active OSHA violations High People capped at 35
CBP labor risk (medium) High People score penalized -15
High greenwashing risk Medium Flagged on brand page

Grade Scale

Scores map to letter grades using these thresholds:

A 80-100 Leading
B 65-79 Good Progress
C 50-64 Getting Started
D 35-49 Needs Work
F 0-34 Failing

Brands with zero data and no red flags are labeled NR (Unranked) rather than receiving an F. An F means we found data and it was bad. NR means we found nothing at all.

Confidence Levels

Every rating includes a confidence indicator based on how many of our 25 possible data indicators had actual data.

  • High Confidence (70%+): 18+ indicators verified. Score is robust.
  • Medium Confidence (40-69%): 10-17 indicators. Score is directionally accurate but may shift with more data.
  • Low Confidence (<40%): Fewer than 10 indicators. Score is based on limited data; treat as preliminary.

Most brands currently have low confidence ratings because sustainability report parsing and OSHA/OAR data collection is ongoing. Scores will become more accurate as we expand data coverage.

Data Sources

ThreadGrade pulls data from the following public, verifiable sources:

Source What It Provides Feeds Into
Fashion Transparency Index Overall supply chain transparency score (0-100%) WHERE dimension
KnowTheChain Benchmark Forced labor due diligence score (0-100) and rank WHO dimension
U.S. Customs & Border Protection Withhold Release Orders, forced labor enforcement WHO dimension + Red Flags
Third-Party Certifications GOTS, OEKO-TEX, Bluesign, B Corp, Fair Trade, etc. WHAT dimension
OSHA (DOL) Workplace safety inspections and violations WHO dimension + Red Flags
Open Supply Hub Factory-to-brand mapping, facility counts by country WHERE dimension
Sustainability Reports AI-parsed brand reports for all dimension indicators All dimensions

Missing Data Policy

When a brand has no data for a dimension, we apply a default score of 25/100. This is deliberately low because:

  • Brands that care about transparency publish data. Silence is not neutral.
  • We refuse to give a brand a passing score just because they haven't been caught doing anything wrong.
  • The burden of proof is on the brand, not the consumer.

This means a brand with zero published data will score around 25/100 (F), while a brand with full transparency and strong practices could score 80+ (A). The gap between them is the "transparency premium" that ThreadGrade was built to surface.

Open Source

Our scoring algorithm is fully open source. You can audit every line of code, every weight, and every data source in our GitHub repository. If you think our methodology is wrong, file an issue. If you have better data, submit a pull request.
