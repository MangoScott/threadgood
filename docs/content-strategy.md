# Content Strategy

## Voice & Tone

ThreadGrade inherits FinMango's voice: direct, data-driven, conversational, no-BS. We don't shame people for their choices. We give them better information within their actual constraints.

**Do:**
- Lead with data and specifics
- Acknowledge systemic problems without blaming individuals
- Compare options at the same price point
- Use plain language, not jargon
- Show your work (cite sources, link to data)

**Don't:**
- Preach or moralize
- Assume readers can afford premium brands
- Use corporate sustainability buzzwords without defining them
- Write em-dash-heavy, AI-sounding copy
- Cherry-pick data to make a point

**Example tone:**
> Not: "Fast fashion is destroying the planet. Here's why you should stop buying from Shein."
>
> Instead: "That $8 tee from Shein costs about $8. Here's what you're not seeing on the price tag, and here's what Target's Goodfellow line does differently at the same price."

## Content Pillars

### 1. Brand Report Cards

The core SEO play. Target the queries people are already searching.

**Format:** ~800-1200 words. Brand overview, ThreadGrade grade with per-dimension breakdown, key data points, what they do well, where they fall short, better alternatives at the same price tier.

**Target keywords:** "Is [brand] ethical", "[brand] sustainability", "[brand] labor practices"

**Priority brands for launch (by US search volume):**
- Shein, Nike, Zara, H&M, Amazon Essentials
- Target (All in Motion, Goodfellow, A New Day)
- Walmart (George, Time and Tru, Wonder Nation)
- Old Navy, Gap, Uniqlo, Levi's
- Fast fashion: Fashion Nova, Forever 21, PrettyLittleThing

### 2. Better At Your Budget

The differentiator content. Nobody else is doing this well.

**Format:** ~1000-1500 words. Pick a category + price tier. Rate every major option. Rank them. Explain why.

**Examples:**
- "The Most Ethical Basics Under $25"
- "Best Ethical Jeans Under $50"
- "Sustainable Activewear That Won't Break the Bank"
- "The Best Ethical Options at Target, Ranked"
- "Every Walmart Clothing Brand, Rated"

### 3. System Stories

Data-driven features that explain why the system works the way it does. FinMango's financial health framing applied to fashion.

**Format:** ~1500-2500 words. Long-form, data-heavy, shareable. Include original data visualizations.

**Examples:**
- "Why Your $8 Tee Actually Costs $32" (hidden costs of fast fashion, with data)
- "The Clothing Desert Problem: Where Americans Can't Shop Ethically Even If They Want To"
- "How US Customs Data Reveals Where Your Clothes Really Come From"
- "The K-Shaped Closet: How Income Inequality Shapes What Americans Wear"

### 4. Data Reports

Annual and quarterly publications that establish authority and generate press coverage.

**Format:** 10-20 page reports with data visualizations, rankings, and analysis.

**Examples:**
- "State of American Ethical Fashion 2026" (annual)
- "Q1 2026: Which Brands Improved? Which Got Worse?"
- "The Greenwashing Report: Brands With the Biggest Gap Between Claims and Data"

### 5. Interactive Tools (Mango Stories)

Building on FinMango's existing interactive module framework.

**Examples:**
- "Follow Your Tee" — interactive supply chain journey from cotton to closet
- "True Cost Calculator" — input what you paid, see estimated real costs (labor, environment, externalities)
- "Ethical Closet Quiz" — personalized recommendations based on budget, style, and values

## Distribution Channels

### SEO/Blog (Primary)
- Target high-intent queries Americans already search
- Brand report cards = core SEO engine
- "Better At Your Budget" = long-tail keyword capture
- Technical SEO: static generation for fast pages, structured data for rich snippets

### Social Media
- **TikTok/Reels**: Short-form content. "I rated every brand at Target." "What your favorite mall brand scored." Visual, shareable.
- **Instagram**: Infographics, grade card visuals, brand comparison carousels
- **X/Twitter**: Data drops, hot takes on fashion industry news

### Email Newsletter
- Weekly digest: new ratings, featured content, better-at-your-budget picks
- Grow list through site CTAs, social, and ambassador network

### FinMango Ambassador Network
- Activate 2023/2024/2025 cohorts for ThreadGrade content
- Provide templates and assets for social posting
- Ambassador challenges: "Rate 5 brands in your closet"

### University Partnerships
- UVA SEED Consulting model: student research teams conduct brand analysis
- Students create case studies, supply chain research, content
- Win-win: students get real research experience, ThreadGrade gets scalable analysis
